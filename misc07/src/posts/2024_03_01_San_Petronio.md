## March 1st – San Petronio
![SanPetronio](/static/images/basilica_san_petronio.png)

They never finished the façade.
You know that, right?

Started it with marble, ended it in brick.
A city caught mid-thought. Or mid-prayer.

Inside, it doesn’t echo like a church.
It listens like a courtroom.
Or a machine.

I counted the columns. I lost count.
Somewhere between the meridian line and the chapel that shouldn’t be there,
I swear I felt the temperature drop.
Not cold — just off.
Like standing inside a question.

The ceiling is too high. The floor too patient.
And the saints…
The saints look tired.

They say the devil once stood here.
They say the Pope was afraid of the sky it tried to touch.
They say a city builds its cathedral not to worship —
but to bury something too large to forget.

And maybe that’s all this is.
A cover. A pause. A structure that pretends to stand still
while something underneath keeps dreaming.

— L.C.
