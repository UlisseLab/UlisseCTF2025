## March 3rd – Via Piella
![ViaPiella](/static/images/finestrella.jpg)


I stood in front of the Finestrella for forty minutes. Maybe more. The water was too still. Like it was listening.

People passed behind me. A couple laughed. They called it cute.

But this isn’t a view. It’s a trap. A fracture. A portal. A canal that shouldn't be visible — and yet there it is, like a memory that refuses to stay buried.

There’s something beneath it. Not physically. Not exactly. But I felt it. Something like a pull — like the city was holding its breath.

And then I remembered what I wrote, weeks ago. Or maybe I didn't write it. Maybe I just received it.

> This window shows a river that shouldn’t be here. A mirror of what’s hidden beneath — not just water, but silence.
> I left a sign not far from here. In a palace of coats and carved ceilings, **where books listen more than they speak**.
> **If you find the crest with no name beneath it, stand below it and look up. You’ll see what I saw. The stars. Inside the wood.**"
> 
> L.C.

Don’t ask me how I know. Just look for the one who wasn’t named.
— L.C.
