## April 15th – San Michele in Bosco
![smb2](/static/images/san-michele-bosco-2.jpg)

You climb for air, they say.
To breathe better.
To see things from above.

But up here the city doesn’t shrink.
It rearranges.
Streets turn into veins, windows into pores.
Everything alive, but slower.

I watched the rooftops for a long time.
At some point they stopped being buildings
and became thoughts I hadn’t had yet.

It’s quiet here.
But not empty.
The trees whisper things the bricks would never admit.
And the wind carries secrets that don’t belong to anyone.

There’s a spot, if you sit still enough,
where Bologna opens herself just for a second —
not to reveal anything,
but to remind you that something’s always been hidden.

And that maybe the higher you go,
the deeper it gets.

— L.C.
