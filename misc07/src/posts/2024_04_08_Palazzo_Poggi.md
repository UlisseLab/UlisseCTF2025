## April 8th – Palazzo Poggi
![PalazzoPoggi](/static/images/palazzo-poggi.jpg)

I came here because they told me this was the heart.
Of reason. Of medicine. Of what they now call “la dotta.”

They say the professors used to sit here,that one of them carved the words “Panum Resis” into the chair. Knowledge is the root of all decision. A clear thought. Too clear, maybe.

No one can find it now. The chair is still here. The carving isn’t.

Erased? Polished away? Or simply never there?
But there was a time when learning didn’t need chairs.

They call this the birthplace now. Of reason. Of structure. Of learning.

And yet, if you listen closely,the walls don’t sound like firsts — **they echo like seconds**. 

As if something older once held the title.
Something that taught before this place was even planned.

But I know it existed.
**An older Studium. The one.**

So I've imagined.
**Fifteen minutes, heading southwest.**
Past the towers. Past the squares.
Just instinct and the weight of forgotten footsteps.

And there it was.

**A building with walls covered in hundreds of family crests.**
**A theatre built entirely in wood, where silence still breathes.**
**A hall where lectures were sung like rituals.**

Something is close.
**And it listens from under a ceiling full of stars.**

If you’ve seen that ceiling,
you already know.

— L.C.
