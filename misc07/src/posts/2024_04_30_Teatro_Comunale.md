## April 30th – Teatro Comunale

![](/static/images/teatro-bologna.jpg)
They tuned in silence.

That was the first thing I noticed.

No coughing. 

No footsteps.

No throat-clearing. 

Just breath being held — like the entire room was waiting to be transmuted.

And when the strings began,
they didn’t vibrate — they opened.
Like veins.

Each note pulled something out of the air.

Something old. Something circular.

I saw a man across the aisle covering his ears.
Too late.

It wasn’t sound.
It was distillation.
Brass became gold.
Tension turned to vapor.
And I swear one of the cellos folded in on itself and became a cube.

This wasn’t a concert.
It was an opus minor.
But the audience thought it was Puccini.

They clapped.
I left.

And for a moment, in the corridor,
I heard someone whisper:
“Solve et coagula.”

But no one was there.

— L.C.
