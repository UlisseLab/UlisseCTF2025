## March 7th, Fontana del Nettuno
![nettuno](/static/images/nettuno.webp)

You don’t notice it at first. They tell you to admire the symmetry, the force, the classic lines. But there’s always something out of place.Or too perfectly placed.

If you stand at the right spot — just beyond the base, looking from the southeast corner of the square —you’ll see it.Not a finger, not really. Not anymore.

It points like a compass. Like it’s trying to say something.The old joke is that it’s a symbol of virility. But I think it’s a symbol of time.

**That finger — that minute hand — is aimed directly toward Via d’Azeglio, a street walked by students and strangers alike, but only a few of them ever bother to look at where the hand is pointing.**

**If the fountain is a clock, then the time is eleven.**

**Eleven o’clock.**

The time before something changes.

They say knowledge doesn’t move. But I think it shifts — in quiet angles and stone gestures.

And between the shadow of the tridentand the echo of those fingers, **the Rose is closer than most imagine.**

But not everyone sees it bloom.

— L.C.
