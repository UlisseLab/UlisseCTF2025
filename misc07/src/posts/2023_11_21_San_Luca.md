## November 21th, 2023, Basilica della Madonna di San Luca, Bologna

![SanLuca](/static/images/San-Luca-Bologna.jpg.webp)

I climbed to the Basilica before dawn. Fog thick as milk. The city vanished below me, like Bologna didn’t exist — only the hill, the silence, and whatever still lingers up here.

They say San Luca watches over the city. I don’t believe in that kind of protection. Not anymore. But I do believe in alignments — of geography, symbols, light, memory. And something’s off here. Or too perfect.

My great-grandfather used to say:
"When the rose blooms where the Mother watches, the fire will speak again."
I thought he was just rambling into his wine. Now I'm not so sure.

There’s a carving behind the left column near the apse — almost erased by time. A double circle with a cross inside. It matches something from the marginalia of Vincenzo’s last notebook. Coincidence? Maybe. But alchemy isn’t built on coincidences. It’s built on patterns.
And fear.

There’s sulfur in the air here. Not the real kind — the kind that sits in your mind, just behind the teeth. Something once happened up here. Maybe they met here. Maybe they hid something beneath the steps. Or maybe I’ve been reading too many dead languages again.


— L.C.
