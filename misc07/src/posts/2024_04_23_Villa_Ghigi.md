## April 23rd – Villa Ghigi

![Custode](/static/images/casa-custode-villa-ghigi.jpg)
![VillaGhigi](/static/images/villa-ghigi.jpg)


I thought this place would be quiet.
It isn’t.

The trees talk.
Not in words — in arrangement.
Some lean too far left. Others cluster like conspirators.
One oak has a scar that looks like an eye, half-open.

The grass is soft, but it shifts when you’re not looking.
Like it’s breathing.
Like the hill itself is trying to roll over without waking anyone.

I sat under a fig tree and watched the city below.
Bologna was still.
But not calm.
It looked like a painting that knew it was being watched.

A blackbird landed near my foot.
It blinked once. Then again, but slower.
Not natural.

There’s a path that curls up near the edge of the woods.
No sign. Just moss.
It goes nowhere.
But I think if you walk it long enough,
you end up inside something.

Not a forest. Not a memory.
Something older than both.

And sweeter.

— L.C.
