## April 25th – Torre Asinelli

![](/static/images//vaso_rotto.png)

April 15th – Top of the Asinelli Tower

They say the vase at the top was just a myth.
But myths, I’ve learned, don’t shatter for no reason.

Today, at the very summit, hidden inside the cracked stone vessel, I found something wedged deep between two shards of ancient terracotta:
a small green glass stopper, dusty, but whole.

Etched onto its surface, barely visible:
"Sub signo"
Under the sign.

According to an old alchemical notebook I’ve been studying — one passed down by a circle of scholars close to Vincenzo Casciarolo — the stopper once belonged to a **student of Medicine who attended the University of Bologna. His name was Marco Celati, and the fragment of parchment bearing his name is dated 1650.**

**They say Marco believed the body could only be properly understood when studied “under the right stars.”** He left no thesis, no degree. Just this note, passed from hand to hand:

> Ho nascosto il mio segno dove il cielo si osserva meglio, ma si guarda poco.
> 
> “I hid my sign where the sky is clearest — but few bother to look.”

And now, centuries later, I find it — not in the stars, but inside a broken vessel, at the highest point of the city.

I don’t think it’s a coincidence.
I think Marco was pointing downward.
**Back to the room beneath the ceiling full of constellations.**

— L.C.
