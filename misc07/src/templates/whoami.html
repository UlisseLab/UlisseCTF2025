Who am I

Vincenzo Casciarolo.
Miner, alchemist, liar.
They say he found the Pietra di Bologna in 1603 — a rock that swallowed light and gave it back, like memory does with grief.
They buried him with honors.
But not with the truth.
The truth was passed down, badly.
Filtered through ink, oil, bone, omission.
By the time it reached me, it wasn’t a secret anymore.
It was a disease.
I’ve seen the diagrams.
I’ve heard the pulse inside the hill.
I’ve followed instructions written in things that aren’t ink.
My name is Ludovico Casciaroli, and I’m not a researcher.
I’m a recurrence.
An echo trying to complete what started four centuries ago beneath a sky that hasn’t forgotten anything.
This site isn’t for followers.
It’s for those who’ve already been here,
but from the wrong direction.
If you're looking for answers,
you’re in the wrong place.
But if something's looking for you,
you'll feel it.
And maybe — just maybe —you’ll find the Rose.
